Study: Doctors aren't only clinicians safe to perform abortions

Jan. 23, 2013, 2:56 p.m.

A new study says other types of clinicians, such as physicians assistants, are just as capable as performing abortions as doctors. (Flickr via Mercy Health)


By Hayley Fox
Comment

Tweet

A new study out of UCSF shows that nurse practitioners (NP), physician assistants (PA) and certified nurse midwives can perform abortions just as safely as doctors.

In California, all of these medical providers are able to provide medication abortions: Women who are less than nine weeks along in their pregnancy can take a pill (often costing $300 to $800), that deteriorates the lining of their uterus and terminates the pregnancy.

Planned Parenthood reports that women who are later in their pregnancy but still less than about four months, are eligible to receive the most common form of abortion; an aspiration abortion, also known as a vacuum aspiration.

As of now, California only allows physicians to perform these type of abortions, and the Golden State is not alone; only four states in the country allow non-physicians to provide aspirations, according to a study published in the American Journal of Public Health.

“Increasing the types of health care professionals who can provide early aspiration abortion care is one way to reduce this health care disparity,’’ said lead study author Tracy Weitz, PhD, MPA, in a statement. “Policy makers can now feel confident that expanding access to care in this way is evidence-based and will promote women’s health.’’

But according to new research, these restrictions may cause more harm than good -- especially for low-income and minority women. The new study states that these demographics are often served by public health departments or community health centers, many of which do not provide abortions. And it is these women who are often seen by NPs and PAs, not doctors.

By allowing more people to perform this common type of abortion, researchers hope to increase overall access to this procedure.

In 2008, 1.21 million abortions took place in the United States, with more than 18 percent of these occurring in California. In this most recent study, 40 NPs, certified nurse midwives and PAs from four Planned Parenthood affiliates and from Kaiser Permanente of Northern California were trained to perform aspiration abortions. They were compared to a group of about 100 physicians, and altogether the two groups performed more than 11,000 abortions.

Researchers concluded that "care provided by newly trained NPs, CNMs, and PAs was not inferior to that provided by experienced physicians. We estimate that only 1 additional complication would occur for every 120 procedures as a consequence of having an NP, CNM, or PA as the abortion provider."

The study went on to clarify that these complications were typically minor, such as infection or pain, and easily resolved.

Researchers concluded,"As the demand for health care providers increases under US health care reform, one part of the solution for all health care, including abortion care, is to allow all qualified professionals to perform clinical care to the fullest extent of their education and competency."
