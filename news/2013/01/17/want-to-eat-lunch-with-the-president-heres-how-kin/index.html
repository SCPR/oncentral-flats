Want to eat lunch with the president? Here's how (kind of)

Jan. 17, 2013, 11:44 a.m.

You can make the same lunch at home that President Obama will be eating on his Inauguration Day. (Flickr via Talk Radio News Service)


By Hayley Fox
Comment

Tweet

On Monday, President Barack Obama -- and Vice President Joe Biden -- will be ceremonially sworn in for another four years as America's leaders. Their day is punctuated by an inaugural parade, ball and luncheon. Although we can't all actually attend the inauguration, we can watch it on TV and eat along with them.

The luncheon menu for Monday's event has been released, along with recipes for each course. Here's how the people on the hill will be eating:

First Course: Steamed Lobster with New England Clam Chowder Sauce
Lobster tails with clam chowder sauce, sautéed spinach and Sweet Potato Hay (shredded sweet potatoes fried in oil). While decadent lobster may be a little heavy for a weekday lunch, the recipe for sauteed spinach is simple and doable.

Ingredients:
- 8 ounces baby spinach
- 1/4 cup shallot, minced
- 1/2 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 pinch sea salt
- 1 pinch cracked black pepper

Directions: Pre-heat a large sauté pan on high heat. Add shallots to oil and cook until tender. Add the spinach and season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook until the spinach is wilted and serve warm.

Second Course: Hickory Grilled Bison with Red Potato Horseradish Cake and Wild Huckleberry Reduction
The bison comes with butternut squash puree, red cabbage and strawberry preserve and baby golden beets with string beans. Because bison may be a little hard to come by, try making the Red Potato Horseradish Cake -- a different incarnation of mashed potatoes.

Ingredients:
-12 ounces red potato, medium sized, cut 1 inch dice, skin on
- 1 tablespoon prepared horseradish (available at most grocery stores)
- 1/2 tablespoon Dijon mustard, whole grain
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 1/2 cup heavy cream (You can use a little less cream to cut down on fat content but expect slightly chunkier potato cakes)
- 2 tablespoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/4 cup chives
- 1/2 gallon water
- 1 cup micro greens, available at specialty markets (for presentation -- these can be omitted)

Directions: Place cubed potatoes in ¼ to ½ gallon of water in a heavy bottom pot. Add 1 tablespoon salt and bring everything to a boil. Let potatoes simmer for 10-15 minutes or until they are tender. Drain water and let potatoes rest in a strainer for 5 minutes. Put the potatoes, Dijon, horseradish, 2 tablespoons butter, heavy cream, salt, pepper and chives back into the original pot you used for boiling. Stir and smash potatoes with a kitchen spoon until everything is mixed but still chunky. Taste and add salt and pepper accordingly.

Using a spoon or an ice-cream scooper, make 3 oz. scoops from the potato mixture and place on a greased baking sheet. Use fingers to make the potatoes into a cake shape and drizzle butter ove the top. Cook potatoes under the broiler for 4 to 5 minutes or until golden brown.

Final Course: Hudson Valley Apple Pie with Sour Cream Ice Cream, Aged Cheese and Honey
Because the sour cream ice cream requires special machinery (an ice cream maker), you can make the homemade pie yourself and buy store-bought ice cream to go with it. Another time-saving shortcut is to buy a pie crust instead of make it yourself; then all you need to do is prepare the apples and the cinnamon crumble.

Ingredients for apples:
- 1 pound apples, peeled, cored, sliced thin
- 3 ounces sugar
- 1/4 tablespoon cinnamon
- 1 1/2 tablespoon corn starch
- 1/4 teaspoon vanilla

Directions for apples: Combine all the above ingredients in a mixing bowl until apples are coated. Layer apples tightly into pie crust all the way up the sides of the shell (the apples will shrink as they're baked). Top the pie with cinnamon crumble and bake at 350º F for 25-30 minutes.

Ingredients for cinnamon crumble:
-1/2 cup all purpose flour
-1/4 cup sugar
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1 pinch salt
- 3 tablespoons butter, unsalted, chilled, cut in to 1/2 inch cubes

Directions: Blend first 5 ingredients in a food processor (you can do this part by hand if you dont have a processor). Add chilled butter cubes; pulse the processor until mixture looks like wet sand. Sprinkle over top of pie -- and/or over ice cream.

To wash down this multi-course meal, two different types of wine and one champagne will be served at the inaugural lunch.

The inauguration will be broadcast on multiple TV channels beginning this weekend. On Monday, NBC has the presidential event on its schedule from 7:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. and CNN will begin covering the event at 6 a.m.
