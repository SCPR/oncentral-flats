Help shape the future of South LA at Community Plan hearing this week

Jan. 14, 2013, 10:06 a.m.

The Community Plans slated for the South L.A. area include limiting the amount of fast food in the area and increasing incentives for fresh food locations.


By Hayley Fox
Comment

Tweet

A South L.A. Community Plan, which will shape development in the area for decades to come, is poised for a City Council vote -- but before the plan is finalized, residents and stakeholders are invited to share their input at an open house and hearing this week.

According to non-profit health policy group, Community Health Councils (CHC), this plan for the Baldwin Hills/West Adams/Leimert Park area, will "guide future development and determine access to healthier foods, parks and open space, public transportation, pedestrian hubs, and bike lanes" for the next 20 to 30 years.

CHC says that although the current West Adams Community Plan "includes many provisions that will enhance the quality of life for South LA residents," it could be doing much more to make the neighborhood public transit and bike friendly, as well as "establishing safeguards to protect the character of our communities."

Critics of the city plan also say that it doesn't highlight the importance of new park space as much as it should, and doesn't adequately address the ongoing issue of too much fast food and not enough fresh food in the area.

As a result, the CHC and a coalition of South L,A. community organizations are encouraging stakeholders and residents to attend a hearing in front of the L.A. Department of City Planning on Tuesday, January 15. An open house begins at 5 p.m. at the Nate Holden Performing Arts Center, followed by the hearing at 7 p.m.

"This hearing provides a rare chance for residents’ comments to be considered in the final report prior to a City Council vote," says the CHC.

The West Adams Community Plan Area is bounded by Pico and Venice Boulevards to the north, Robertson Boulevard and Culver City to the west, the Baldwin Hills, unincorporated Los Angeles County and the City of Inglewood to the southwest, and Arlington and Van Ness Avenues to the east.

This area is one of three located within the City’s South Los Angeles Planning Region -- which also includes the Southeast Community Plan and the South L.A. Community Plan.

These three districts have identified many similar issues as the focus of their new community plans. South L.A.'s Community Plan which encompasses the area of council districts 5 and 10, also emphasizes the need for accessible public transit and supports more restrictions on neighborhood "nuisances" such as liquor stores and motels.

The L.A. Department of City Planning will be at Tuesday's event and emphasize that the Community Plan is a "collaborative effort between residents, stakeholders and the business and development community."

Nate Holden Performing Arts Center is located at 4718 West Washington Blvd, LA 90016
