Messy break-ups, bar-bending and knife-wielding sons: This Week in Newton

March 2, 2012, 5:50 p.m.

The headquarters of the Los Angeles Police Department's Newton Division. (Credit: Google Maps)


By José Martinez
OnCentral
Comment

Tweet

In "This Week in Newton," OnCentral recaps some of the week's occurrences in Newton Division of the Los Angeles Police Department.

Newton Police Division Boundaries


View Newton Police Division in a larger map

For the period beginning with February 26 and ending on March 2, incidents within the division included:

February 26, 2012 at approximately 9 a.m. 4800 block of Ascot Avenue: A 21-year-old male suspect became involved in a verbal dispute with his 50-year-old mother and attempted to stab her with a knife, threatening to kill her during the incident. He was arrested.

February 26, 2012 at approximately 10:30 a.m. Intersection of 54th and Main Streets: A 49-year-old woman returned to her car, which she had parked in a store's lot while shopping, and returned to discover that the front right tire was missing.

February 26, 2012 at approximately 7 p.m. 5400 block of Duarte Street: A black 15-year-old male took his aunt's ATM card and was caught on camera about an hour later withdrawing money from his aunt's account. He has black hair and brown eyes, weighs approximately 150 pounds and stands at 5'09". He still at large and no arrests were made.

February 26, 2012 at approximately 10 p.m. Intersection of 43rd Street and Avalon Boulevard: A black male pointed a gun at a victim's head and demanded his vehicle. The suspect, who has black hair and brown eyes, fled in the victim's vehicle, a white 2005 Toyota Corolla. He is still at large and no arrests were made.

February 28, 2012 at approximately 5 a.m. Intersection of 47th Street and Broadway: A male approached his car, which was parked on the street, to find that his passenger window had been broken and that merchandise he had left in his car was missing. Police have no information about the suspect, who is still at large. No arrests were made.

February 28, 2012 at approximately 5 p.m. 200 block of West 40th Place: An 18-year-old male who was being chased by the police after running from a routine pedestrian stop ran behind an apartment building and attempted to force open the steel security bars on a rear window using brute force. The police caught up to him and arrested before he could enter the premises.

February 28, 2012 between approximately 4 p.m. and 10 p.m. 6100 block of South Broadway: After checking into a hotel with a long-time male friend, a 35-year-old Hispanic female with brown hair and brown eyes stole her friend's money after he fell asleep. She stands at about 5'08" and weighs around 180 pounds and has tattoos near her right eye. She is still at large and no arrests were made.

February 29, 2012 at approximately 2 p.m. Intersection of Vernon and Hooper Avenues: A black 18-year-old male, upset over a break-up, began to shoot at his 39-year-old ex-girlfriend while she was sitting in her car. The suspect, standing at 5'09" and weighing about 155 pounds, had gang tattoos on both of his arms and fled in a black Nissan Altima. He is still at large and no arrests were made.

February 29, 2012 at approximately 3 p.m. 4000 block of Woodlawn Avenue: An 18-year-old black female was in a verbal dispute with her 74-year-old neighbor, who was under the influence, when she produced a kitchen knife and stabbed him in the chest twice. She has black hair and brown eyes. She is still at large and no arrests were made.

February 29, 2012 at approximately 4 p.m. Intersection of Maple Avenue and Washington Boulevard: A 43-year-old male discovered that his car, the doors of which had been unlocked, had been entered and that its A/C climate control system had been stolen. Police have no information about the suspect, who is still at large. No arrests were made.

March 1, 2012 at approximately 2 p.m. Intersection of Tarleton Street and Washington Boulevard: Two Hispanic males broke into a vehicle using a lock slip, took property from inside the vehicle and then fled in a grey Nissan Altima. The first suspect was between 32 and 37 years old, between 215 and 230 pounds and bald. The second suspect was between 215 and 230 pounds. Both suspects are still at large and no arrests were made.
