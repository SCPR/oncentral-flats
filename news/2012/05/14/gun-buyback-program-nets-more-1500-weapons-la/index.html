Gun buyback program nets more than 1600 weapons in LA

May 14, 2012, 1:08 p.m.

(credit: David Starkopf) This year's gun buyback program netted more than 1,600 weapons.


By Hayley Fox
Comment

Tweet

More than 1,600 weapons were turned in last weekend as part of L.A.'s gun buyback program, which included six locations throughout L.A. -- one on South Central Avenue.

Fifty-three assault weapons, 791 handguns, 527 rifles and 302 shotguns were handed over to police on Sunday with no questions asked. In exchange, the weapons-owners received a $100 or $200 Visa or Ralphs gift cards.

“If you do the math, we figure there’s about 3 million guns in the city of Los Angeles, but these are guns that people are willing to exchange for groceries,” said Chief Charlie Beck, according to CBS.

Beck added that since the buyback program began four years ago, 7,942 firearms have been turned in, KPCC reports.

This year's event was held on Mothers Day weekend, which officials hoped would spur more people to turn in their weapons.

"We do it on Mother's Day because we think mothers have a strong influence on families and will get them to turn in their unwanted guns," LAPD Chief Charlie Beck said, according to KTLA.

The gun buyback program is an initiative created by the mayor's Gang Reduction and Youth Development (GRYD) Office. It kicks off the city's "Summer Violence Reduction" strategy which also includes the Summer Night Lights program.

KPCC reports that all the guns gathered at Saturday's event will be shredded and used by artist Victor Hugo to create sculptures.
