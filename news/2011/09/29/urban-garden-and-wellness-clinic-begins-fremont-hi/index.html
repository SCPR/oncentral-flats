Urban garden and wellness clinic planned for Fremont High

Sept. 29, 2011, 9:45 p.m.

South L.A residents gather to celebrate community driven reform.


By Josanta Gray
Comment

Tweet

Students, parents, and community members celebrated a new community partnership that would bring an urban community garden and a 2,500 square-foot community clinic to South L.A. at John C. Fremont High School Thursday.

“It’s a beautiful night,” said Alberto Retana. “This is about the community coming together.”

As a collective, the Los Angeles Neighborhood Land Trust, UMMA Clinic, Community Coalition, and Fremont High School are responsible for this one-of-a-kind wellness project. The project is meant to bring comprehensive wellness services to the community including green space, healthy eating, herbal medicine and traditional health services. It is expected to be completed by mid-2012.

“We are taking a minute to celebrate but we got to continue to have community meetings,” said Community Coalition President & CEO Marqueece Harris-Dawson. “[These community meetings] are a good opportunity to get our word in. At the meetings, we try to engage in deep meaningful communication, like what are the things our students need to be successful? Some of that is a wellness center.”

A student poll reported one in four students believe there is no safe space on campus. One in five students said they went to the restroom when they were feeling stressed. Community members recognized that there was a dire need for green space and wellness in South L.A., said Alinda Bokde, the Executive Director of the Los Angeles Neighborhood Land Trust. As a result, they set out design and implement these changes into their community.

According to the Community Coalition, attending to children’s mental health is essential to their ability to grow into healthy, caring, and productive members of society. The wellness program is meant to provide dropout prevention and psychosocial support to students in order to enhance their personal well-being and academic performance.

Senior Paola Lopez desperately needed a place to go to when her father passed away. She believes the wellness center will benefit many students and community members during emotionally demanding times.

“Tonight is about celebration and change,” Lopez said. “Not all students have a bond with a staff member or feel comfortable talking to them. It’s not that [the faculy] doesn’t care, but they’re not trained to support us emotionally.”

“I didn’t feel comfortable talking to adults,” junior Lilly Washington said. “I turned to my friends. I was lucky my friends gave me positive advice. Others are not as lucky.”

As the group collectively shouted, “our future, our Fremont,” this celebration and community meeting marked the beginning of the future for Fremont High School students and South L.A. residents.

“We started with wellness but we still need additional resources to sustain what we’ve started,” said Fremont High School Principal Raphael Balderas.
