Etta James' son kicks off jazz festival with City Council performance

July 27, 2012, 10:26 a.m.

Donto (left) and Sametto James, sons of the late Etta James, were present at Friday morning's City Council meeting. Councilwoman Jan Perry honored of the partners and supporters of the Central Avenue Jazz Festival. Donto opened the session with a "sneak peek" performance. (José Martinez/OnCentral)


By José Martinez
OnCentral
Comment

Tweet

Councilwoman Jan Perry of the ninth district honored supporters and partners of the 17th annual Central Avenue Jazz Festival at Friday morning's City Council session, saying the avenue's cultural character was "still alive and kicking."

Perry, who's headed the district that's the home of the festival since 2001, said she was proud to back the event.

"Central Avenue has long been the historic epicenter for jazz on the West Coast," she said, noting that 35,000 people attend the festival over its two days.

Also present was Donto James, the son of the late Etta James, who died earlier this year. James played drums for his mother most of his life and has two Grammys under his belt. He's currently working on another album, where he'll mark a transition from drummer to frontman. He'll perform a tribute to his mother at the jazz festival with the band Sons of Etta.

"I just feel very fortunate to do this," James said to OnCentral. "It's great – it still hasn't set in yet. But I just feel very fortunate to do this. I know she's looking down. I hope I make her proud."

James, backed by three singers, opened the council meeting with an energetic performance that Perry said was a "little sampling of what you could experience this weekend."

The Central Avenue Jazz Festival harkens back to a time when the thoroughfare was a rich musical hub for the entire city, attracting the likes of Duke Ellington, Louis Armstrong and Billie Holiday to its exclusive Dunbar Hotel.

"The jazz festival features performers from all walks of life, united in their love of jazz," said Perry. "I encourage everyone to come to 42nd and Central this weekend for an incredible time."

The festival runs from 11 a.m. to 7 p.m. on Saturday and Sunday at the intersection of Central Avenue and 42nd Street – right near the Dunbar – and will include performances by Sons of Etta, The Ray Goren Band and Poncho Sanchez.

It's free to the public and there will be food available to purchase. For more information click here.
