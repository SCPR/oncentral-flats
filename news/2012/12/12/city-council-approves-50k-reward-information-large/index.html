UPDATED: City Council approves $50K reward for information on at-large shooter

Dec. 12, 2012, 1:19 p.m.

Police say the shooting took place near the intersection of Hooper and Vernon avenues around 1:20 a.m. on Dec. 9. (Screenshot via Google Maps)


By José Martinez
OnCentral
Comment

Tweet

UPDATE 4:27 p.m. Detective Richard Arciniega of Newton Division said the suspects "may be gang members" but noted that "the victim and her friends were not gang members at all."

Arciniega said Garcia and her friends were walking back from a party when they were approached by three suspects – two of whom were on bikes, and one of which was the shooter. The shooter accosted Garcia's male companion and started patting him down.

"It was basically a robbery," said Arciniega.

Garcia protested that her friend didn't have anything, and after the suspect determined that was true, he "pulled out a gun and shot Jessica in the head."

PREVIOUSLY: The L.A. City Council has approved a $50,000 reward for information leading to the arrest of a man who allegedly shot a 14-year-old girl early Sunday morning.

Jessica Garcia was walking with a group of friends on Dec. 9 near the intersection of Hooper and Vernon avenues when police say she was shot by an unidentified Latino male around 1:20 a.m.

Garcia survived, but remains in critical condition at a local hospital.

Councilwoman Jan Perry, who oversees the district in which the alleged shooting occurred, called it a "devastating and senseless crime," and said in a statement that she was confident the public could help authorities "find and prosecute the person responsible." Perry was the one to introduce the reward to the rest of the council.

Police believe the suspect has black or dark brown hair, weighs around 120 pounds and stands somewhere between 5-foot-5 and 5-foot-7. At the time of the shooting, he was apparently dressed in a dark hoodie and dark pants.

As of press time, officials didn't have a sketch of the suspect.

Authorities are asking anyone with information to call Newton Detectives at (323) 846-6556 or (323) 846-6579. Outside of regular business hours, you can call 1-(877)-527-3247. You can also leave an anonymous tip at 1-(800)-222-8477.
