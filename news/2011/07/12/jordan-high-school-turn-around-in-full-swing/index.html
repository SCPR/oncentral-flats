COLUMN: Jordan High School turn-around in full swing

July 12, 2011, 1:31 p.m.


By Luz Maria Castellanos
Comment

Tweet

After years of being ranked as one of lowest performing school in Los Angeles, a sagging 35% graduation rate, and low Academic Performance Index (API) score, it's time to return Jordan High School to its days of greatness when the school turned out students like Olympic greats Florence Griffith-Joyner and Kevin Curtis Young, and Nobel prize winning Chemist Glenn Seaborg.

This month "Partnership for Los Angeles Schools" assumed control over half of the high school to begin the tough task of turning around the beleaguered campus. The Partnership took the first step by appointing education veteran and community leader Sherri Williams, who prior to Jordan was Principal at 99th St. where she led the school to increases of 106 points on California’s API, dramatically increased parent engagement, and reached out to bring the whole community into 99th Street.

As part of the Partnership's ramp-up to the first day of school, on July 14th the new Jordan will begin its transformation with help from Revolution Prep, an education software and services provider, which every year affirms its social mission by partnering with a local school to conduct a day of on-campus service. This year, Revolution Prep is teaming up with the Partnership and Sherri to help improve the facilities at Jordan High. There will be hundreds of people lending a helping hand – volunteers from Revolution Prep, the Partnership, Jordan High School, parents, students and the community at large.

Revolution Prep is literally closing down for the day and flying in 80-100 of its employees from around the country to join its 80 or so employees in LA to dedicate a full day to revamping the campus with projects that include landscaping, installing new equipment and repainting the boys and girls recreation areas, building picnic tables, painting an inspirational wall, and the creation of a gigantic world atlas in the quad area to name a few of the projects planned for the all-day event. (To see photos from past service days click here: http://insidetherevolution.revolutionprep.com/category/community-activities/service-days/)

I would like to invite you to come to our day of service to meet the new Jordan principal, Sherri Williams, and see first hand the campus' transformation. The day of service will include a press conference with representatives from the City, LAUSD, Revolution Prep, Partnership for LA Schools and local community leaders. (The list of speakers is being finalized).
