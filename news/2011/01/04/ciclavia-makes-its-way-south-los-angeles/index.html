CicLAvia makes its way to South Los Angeles

Jan. 4, 2011, 2:52 p.m.

Cyclists took over the streets on 10/10/10's CicLAvia event. Photo by Gary Leonard/CicLAvia


By Doris Quintanilla
Comment

Tweet

Non-profit organizations, community members and residents joined organizers of CicLAvia in a meeting Dec. 9 to discuss possible bike routes and landmarks in South Los Angeles that would be a part of an event that would allow people to enjoy the streets car-free.

"A couple of non-profits and individuals came to us actually," said Joe Litton, a representative from CicLAvia. "There was a lot of excitement [in the meeting], and many were very much like well, why can't we have one right now?"

The first CicLAvia event is scheduled to take place on April of this year. But due to the planning, preparation and promotion that go into the event, Litton said they would not be able to take CicLAvia to South Los Angeles until the second or third planned event of the year.

"The next step is an exploration bike ride to scope the area," said Litton in regards to putting together a CicLAvia in South Los Angeles. "We have tried to schedule an event to bike on Sundays but have had to cancel it due to the rain." Possible landmarks and streets that were discussed in the meeting include: Compton Creek, Mercado La Paloma, Dunbar Hotel, Central Avenue, Jefferson Boulevard and Slauson Avenue.

Once they have seen the area and made more concrete decisions as to what landmarks and neighborhoods would be a part of the event, Litton said they would put together a host committee that will help in the decision making process, finalize a route, assist in community outreach and fundraising.

While CicLAvia has been approached by several individuals from other parts of Los Angeles that are interested in having a CicLAvia in their neighborhood, Litton said South Los Angeles fits the organization's criteria and will be one of the first areas to take part in the expansion.

"Eventually we would love to do all of Los Angeles," Litton said. "But South L.A. has been neglected by both private and public money in the past and there are not enough parks in the area. They would benefit greatly from the event."

The expansion of CicLAvia events all over Los Angeles comes after the organization's successful 10-10-10 event, where a car-free 7.5-mile bike route was allotted for Angelenos to bike, walk, skate and enjoy other forms of alternative transportation. About 600,000 people attended the event that promoted public health, alternative transportation, open space and closed streets from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.

CicLAvia was inspired by Ciclovia, a weekly 80-mile street closure event in Bogota, Colombia. The highly popular event in Colombia has also inspired programs in Mexico, Quito, Melbourne and Ecuador.

For those who would like to get involved with CicLAvia, Litton suggested to check out their Facebook page and website.
