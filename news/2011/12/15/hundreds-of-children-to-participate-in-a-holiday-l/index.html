EVENT: Hundreds Of Children To Participate In A Holiday Learning Event In Exposition Park

Dec. 15, 2011, 10:50 a.m.


By Terri Dixon
Comment

Tweet

The California African American Museum (CAAM) will host the “Holiday Gift of Learning” event, Saturday, December 17, 2011, 8 a.m. to 4 p.m., where close to 800 children from 13 Boys and Girls Clubs in Los Angeles, schools, community centers and social service organizations will tour the museums in Exposition Park and the Los Angeles Coliseum. Starting and ending at CAAM, the children will tour CAAM, the California Science Center, the Natural History Museum of LA County and the LA Coliseum.

Addition by Terri:

By groups of 200, the first group of children will be led through Exposition Park and welcomed by the majestic sounds of Los Angeles Lawyers Philharmonic under the baton of founder-conductor Gary S. Greene, Esq.

welcoming the first groups, along with remarks by Councilwoman Jan Perry. The children will get the opportunity to learn about the African Diaspora and African American artists of the 1940s – 1980s as they tour CAAM’s latest exhibitions, “The African Diaspora in the Art of Miguel Covarrubias: Driven By Color, Shaped By Cultures” and “Places of Validation, Art & Progression ” - a part of the Getty’s Pacific Standard Time (PST) initiative. They will also explore CAAM’s “Gallery of Discovery,” where they will learn about the history of slavery in the Americas and hear the stories of slaves.

At the California Science Center the children can get a glimpse of how living things can change their environment in their latest exhibition “Ecosystems” and explore the “Creative World” exhibition, which showcases advances humans make to fill the needs for communications, structures and transportation. After exploring the California Science Center, the groups will head to the Natural History Museum, to learn about fossils and see large dinosaur exhibits as they soar high and low in the galleries. They will also get up close and personal with the world’s most bizarre and common insects. KCBS Sportscaster Jim Hill will speak to several of the groups on the history of the LA Coliseum and Sports Arena as the youngsters will be able to peek into the Coliseum.

Lunch will be provided for the young attendees by donations from Domino’s Pizza and The Patina Restaurant Group, refreshments from the Coca Cola Company, ice cream from Nestle Dreyer’s Foundation and Baskin Robbins Training Center, water donated from Nestle Arrowhead, drinks from Carnation Essential Breakfast Company and ice from Union Ice. KABC and “The Spark of Love” campaign and the Staples Center Foundation will be providing gifts for the gift giveaway in CAAM’s Courtyard prior to everyone’s departure. Included in the giveaway bags will be space and science educational material provided by NASA.

CAAM hosts dozens of educational programs throughout the year; all CAAM programs and exhibitions are free and open to the public. For more information on CAAM visit www.caamuseum.org or call (213) 744-7432.

About the California African American Museum
The California African American Museum (CAAM) researches, collects, preserves and interprets for public enrichment the history, art and culture of African Americans. Chartered by the California State Legislature in 1977, CAAM is a state supported institution and a partner with the 501(c)(3) non- profit organization Friends, the Foundation of the California African American Museum. In addition to its permanent collection of over 6,000 objects of art, artifacts and historical documents, CAAM also houses a research library containing more than 20,000 volumes, employs and trains high school students through its Young Docents program, and hosts in-house curated exhibitions and traveling shows on a regular basis. The museum also tours CAAM exhibitions throughout California and the nation.

Located at 600 State Drive in Exposition Park, regular museum public hours are Tuesday-Saturday 10 a.m. - 5 p.m., and Sundays 11 a.m. – 5 p.m. Museum admission and workshop participation is always free. Parking in the adjacent lot is $10 all day at Figueroa and 39th Streets. For more information on the California African American Museum visit www.caamuseum.org.
