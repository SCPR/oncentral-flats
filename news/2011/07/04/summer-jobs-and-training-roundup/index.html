Need a job? A list of summer programs that are hiring

July 4, 2011, midnight


By Derrick Taruc
Comment

Tweet

It's getting harder than ever for the youth of South L.A. to find summer jobs.

The Los Angeles' community development department predicts that 6,000 youth from ages 16 to 24 who were employed last summer will be unemployed this summer. But despite this, there are still summer programs offering opportunities for job training and employment.

OneSource Youth Opportunity System
OneSource Youth Opportunity Center is open to young people 16-24 and provide employment and work readiness skills, career exploration and guidance, computer training and more. Program services such as employment readiness, career guidance, occupational skills training and computer training are offered for free.

To qualify you must be 16 to 21 years old, resident of the City of Los Angeles and income eligible. Come into office or call to find out about orientation which is happening this week. Southeast LA-Watts WorkSource Center, 10950 S. Central Ave., Los Angeles, (323) 563-4702.

Los Angeles Department of Water and Power's Youth Service Academy
The Youth Service Academy helps “at-risk” students prepare for education following high school. Participating students receive a paid part-time job, a LADWP mentor and a life skills class. Jobs are available mainly at LADWP.

Only LAUSD students living in the city of Los Angeles are eligible to participate. According to its website, students interested in participating should contact their counselor. Call LADWP Youth Service Academy Office at (213) 367-1976 or 1-800-FOR-A-JOB.

HIRE LA’s Youth Employment Program
HIRE LA’s Youth provides employment opportunities, summer jobs, work experience and internships to youth and young adults.

All youth are eligible to participate in this program. HIRE LA’s Youth 16-24 Program is open for youth to apply online.

Los Angeles Youth Opportunity Movement
Youth Opportunity provides GED classes, training and skills development, including basic skills classes, GED preparation, resume writing, computer literacy and more to prepare for higher education, job training or workplace entry and long-term employment.

Eligibility requirements are proof of citizenship/residency (residency card, work permit, U.S. Birth Certificate or Passport), social security card, picture ID, live in Los Angeles, be between the ages 14-21 and meet income guidelines. Call to make an appointment at (323) 971-7640.

All People's Christian Center's Workforce Development
The program is open to those who live in the Vernon Central area (90011 zip code) and do not have a high school diploma or GED. The program offers workshops such as career goal setting, workplace communication and etiquette, how to interview and create a resume, cover letter and thank you letter as well as how to make use of job search resources.

Candidates must be between the ages of 17- 25. Candidates with disabilities are welcome if the disability(s) does not hamper their progress in the program. (213) 747-6357 or e-mail Sharon Ramos at sramos@allpeoplescc.org

Los Angeles Unified School District's FirstBreak
The LAUSD's program that helps students find part-time or full-time employment and internships. The website is currently unavailable but their phone line is open. Students or parents can call their office at (213) 241-0878.

To obtain work permits go to the LAUSD Central Office which can be reached at (213) 241-7053. When applying for a work permit, the student needs to bring the following documents and forms:


  • Completed Statement of Intent to Employ Minor and Request for Work Permit (B1-1) form which needs to be signed both by a parent and the employer.

  • A current ID card (i.e. school, DMV, passport)

  • Proof of birthdate (i.e. birth certificate, ID with birthdate on it, official school record with birthdate on it)
