LA gun buyback moved up in wake of Newtown shooting

Dec. 18, 2012, 1:30 p.m.

A photo from May's gun buyback event. (credit: David Starkopf)


By Hayley Fox
Comment

Tweet

A gun buyback program that allows Angelenos to trade in guns for gift cards has been moved from its original May date to next week as a result of the school shooting in Connecticut that left 26 people dead.

"Cities and states must join with the federal government to do everything we can, as quickly as we can, to keep our communities safe," said Mayor Antonio Villaraigosa in a statement. "It is absolutely critical to provide Angelenos with concrete actions they can take today to make our City safer tomorrow."

The annual gun buyback is run by the Mayor's Gang Reduction and Youth Development Office (GRYD) and the LAPD with the goal of getting guns off the street by offering a reward for their surrender -- no questions asked.

On December 26 from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m., people can turn in their firearm and receive a Ralph's gift card in exchange. Assault weapons are worth a $200 gift card; handguns, rifles and shotguns are worth $100. The gun buyback will be set up at two locations in the city including one in South L.A. This station will be located in Parking Lot 6 at the L.A. Sports Arena near the intersection of Martin Luther King, Jr. Blvd. and Figueroa.

The last gun buyback was in May of of this year and netted more than 1,600 weapons at six different trade-in locations. Since the program began police have collected nearly 8,000 firearms across the city.

Locations for next week's gun buyback include:

L.A. Sports Arena
3939 South Figueroa Street
Los Angeles, CA 90037

Van Nuys Masonic Temple
14750 Sherman Way
Van Nuys, CA 91405
