Berry benefits – and the end of the pubic louse? In health news today

Jan. 15, 2013, 9:03 a.m.

The pubic louse, pictured above, may be on the brink of extinction thanks to grooming practices that leave folks with little or no hair down there. (CDC)


By José Martinez
OnCentral
Comment

Tweet

California's done a pretty good job of avoiding the brunt of flu season, reports the Los Angeles Times – but that probably won't be the case for long. L.A. is "on the upswing" of the virus' season, and health officials continue to urge Angelenos to get vaccinated as soon as they can.

Plenty of people are letting that advice go unheeded, however – particularly for their kids. HealthDay says between 2004 and 2009, less than 45 percent of young children in three counties in Ohio, New York and Tennessee received a flu shot.

In the same vein, KPCC has tips for folks trying to stay healthy during this sickly season.

Prescription drug abuse is a big problem in Los Angeles County, according to a new report from the Department of Public Health – the region saw more than 8,200 drug-related deaths between 2000 and 2009, 60 percent of which involved commonly abused prescription or over-the-counter drugs. Along with its report, KPCC says the department issued a set of recommendations to begin addressing the problem.

Bloomberg reports on a new Pew survey that shows that 41 percent of respondents who went online to look for medical information had their self-diagnoses validated by a physician later on. On the other hand, 18 percent of patients who attempted self-diagnosis said their doctors later told them what they found on the Internet was wrong. The takeaway? There's plenty of good information on the web – you've just to find it.

A new study suggests that children and teenagers who watch TV, play video games or use the computer before they fall asleep are likely to take longer to doze off than their counterparts who don't get any pre-bedtime screen time. HealthDay says that could mean a loss of up to an hour of sleep every week, which one expert notes has "all sorts of detrimental consequences."

A new analysis is challenging a previous study's findings that marijuana use lowers IQ, says U.S. News & World Report, although the author himself is cautioning that his theory may not hold up very well.

If you are a woman who likes berries, you'll also like new research reported on by the Times: It seems younger women who eat at least three servings of strawberries or blueberries a week reduce their likelihood of a heart attack by one-third, compared to women who don't eat many berries.

Doctors continue to join the 21st century: HealthDay says more than two-thirds of family physicians now use electronic health records, meaning the percentage who do so doubled between 2005 and 2011.

And finally, the Guardian asks the question we've all been wondering about: Are pubic lice in danger of extinction? Some are suggesting they are, thanks in part to grooming practices that leave one's nether regions with little or no hair.
